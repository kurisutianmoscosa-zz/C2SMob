SAM-1™ Sensors

ORP (REDOX), pH and Conductivity sensors have a limited lifespan. To get the most out of your sensors, these procedures can help.

Storage

Always store your ORP (REDOX) and pH sensor with the glass bulb in an appropriate solution. Sensorex provides our S016 pH sensor storage solution in a soaker bottle with new sensors. The pH sensors should never be allowed to dry out. If you do not have a storage solution available, pH 4.0 buffer can be used. Never store the sensor in de-ionized or distilled water.  Your conductivity sensor should be rinsed with distilled or deionized water and kept dry when not in use.

Protection

Most sensors are sold with protective caps or bottles that meet the storage requirements and provide mechanical protection. The platinum wire or disk of the ORP sensor and the glass bulb on a pH sensor is very fragile, and should always be covered when not in use.

Cleaning

A sensor may become fouled, and require cleaning. Cleaning solutions are available and will help remove deposits of proteins or other materials from the glass bulb or conductive surfaces. Follow the manufacturer's directions for using these solutions. Cleaning will not help in the case where the reference cell has become contaminated. Do not use abrasives to clean the sensor since this can adversely affect the measurement accuracy.

Contamination

The reference cell of a combination sensor can become contaminated. This condition usually means that the sensor must be discarded. To protect against contamination, make sure that you are using an sensor that is appropriate for the solutions being measured. Double junction sensors have a second barrier to limit contamination and are recommended for solutions with high ion concentrations. Don't leave the sensor in the solution longer than required to take the measurement and rinse the sensor then store in a storage solution similar to our S016 pH sensor storage solution after each use.
